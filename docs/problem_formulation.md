# Problem Formulation: Jeopardy Data Analysis

## Research questions

1. How often does the correct answer appear in the question text (answer-in-question), and could this be used as a simple strategy?
2. Do high-value questions reuse wording from past questions (question overlap) more than low-value ones?
3. Are certain terms associated with high-value vs low-value questions in a statistically significant way (chi-squared test)?

## Success criteria

- **Primary:** Reproducible analysis with effect sizes and chi-squared tests; report confidence intervals or p-values with multiple-test correction (e.g. Bonferroni) where applicable.
- **Baseline:** Empirical marginal rates (e.g. overall proportion of answer-in-question, overall term frequencies).
- **Documentation:** Data scope, vintage, and limitations in a data sheet.

## Stakeholders and decisions

- **Trivia creators / quiz design:** Insights for question design and difficulty.
- **Portfolio:** Demonstrates hypothesis-driven EDA, chi-squared, and reproducible pipeline.
